It depends on your goals. For maximum physical scratch and rock chip protection, PPF is best. If your goal is extreme chemical resistance, gloss, and ease of cleaning, 10H Graphene or 9H Ceramic Coating is ideal.

A professional grade 9H/10H ceramic coating typically lasts between 3 to 7 years, depending on the number of layers applied and your adherence to routine maintenance washing guidelines.

For highway driving where rock chips are a concern, PPF is necessary. Many luxury car owners opt for full-body PPF with a ceramic coating layered on top to get both impact protection and self-cleaning gloss.

Yes, our curated selection of maintenance kits (shampoos, detailers, towels) are available for purchase. However, professional 10H coatings and PPF rolls are strictly for studio application only to ensure warranty validity.

Yes, all protective films and ceramic coatings applied inside our studios come with official registered warranties ranging from 3 to 10 years covering yellowing, peeling, and loss of gloss.

Wash every two weeks using a pH-neutral snow foam shampoo and the two-bucket method. Avoid automated brush washes completely. Use a silica-infused detailing spray every few months to boost the hydrophobic layer.

Wait 7 days before the first wash to let the edges cure. Keep high-pressure washer nozzles at least 18 inches away from film edges. Remove bird droppings immediately to prevent acid etching into the TPU layer.

Maintenance products like shampoos and waxes are DIY-friendly. However, nano-ceramic coatings, graphene matrices, and PPF require rigorous paint correction, clean-room environments, and IR curing, demanding professional installation.

Graphene introduces an atom-thin carbon lattice that significantly lowers the surface temperature of the panel. This provides better heat dissipation than standard ceramics, making it far more resistant to hard water spotting on dark cars.
