Paint Flaw Index

Professional paint correction removes swirl marks, scratches, oxidation, holograms, water spots, and other paint imperfections to restore your vehicle's showroom finish.

Swirl Marks

Fine circular scratches caused by improper washing techniques. Our multi-stage paint correction safely removes swirl marks and restores deep gloss without damaging the factory clear coat.

Light Scratches

Minor scratches from daily use, keys, bags, or improper cleaning can reduce your vehicle's appearance. Professional polishing significantly minimizes these defects for a smoother finish.

Oxidation

Sun exposure gradually fades paint and reduces shine. Paint correction removes oxidation and restores the original depth, colour, and clarity of your vehicle's finish.

Water Spots

Mineral deposits left by hard water can permanently etch into the paint. Our correction process removes stubborn water spot marks before they cause long-term damage.

Holograms

Poor polishing techniques often leave holograms and buffer trails. Our finishing polish eliminates these imperfections, leaving behind a crystal-clear reflection.

Bird Dropping Damage

Corrosive localized paint etching tracks that occur when aggressive uric acids react with hot clear coats.

Tree Sap Marks

Hardened sticky organic sap resins that bond with paint panels, leaving behind dark chemical ring spots.

Paint Dullness

Over time, contaminants and micro-scratches reduce paint clarity. Paint correction restores a rich, glossy finish that makes your vehicle look showroom new.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our luxury paint correction options.

Paint correction is the mechanical leveling of your car's exterior clear coat using specialized compounding machines, fine diminishing abrasives, and tailored leveling pads. It permanently eliminates paint swirls, light scratches, and oxidation to achieve maximum reflection clarity.

Vehicles lose their original shine due to microscopic clear coat damage. Environmental factors like solar UV weathering, road salt sprays, tree sap acids, and abrasive hand washing introduce fine scratches that scatter light reflections instead of allowing them to pass through clearly.

Swirl marks are circular micro-scratches left on clear coat surfaces by poor washing techniques. Wiping your car with dirty sponges, dusty cloths, or using automatic tunnel wash brushes tracks grit across panels, carving thousands of fine circular diffraction lines.

Holograms, often called buffer trails, are hazy patterns left across your clear coat by untrained detailers using high-speed rotary buffing machines with dirty wool pads. They appear as shifting, oily 3D shadows under direct sun rays.

Oxidation occurs when solar radiation systematically breaks down original automotive polymers, allowing atmospheric oxygen to react with your clear coat. This leaves behind a cloudy, chalky, or white finish across the body panels.

Standard polishing or waxing temporarily masks defects using cheap silicone fillers that wash away after a few rains. True paint correction uses precision micro-abrasives to permanently level down clear coat imperfections, permanently restoring optical clarity.
